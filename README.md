# Incygames

Building simple, useful and occasionally game-inspired software.

## About

**Incygames** is an independent software company founded by Phil Martin. The company develops digital products, mobile apps, websites and experiments that combine practical utility with engaging user experiences.

The philosophy behind Incygames is:

> Build products that solve real problems, validate them quickly and improve them through user feedback.

Projects range from consumer applications and websites to games, productivity tools and AI-powered products.

---

## Current Projects

### Daily View

A simple display system designed to help people keep track of their day.

Daily View provides a clear, always-visible screen showing:

* Current date and time
* Time of day (Morning, Afternoon, Evening, Night)
* Upcoming events
* Important messages
* Family updates

The goal is to reduce confusion and improve confidence, particularly for older adults, people living independently, and care environments.

Website: [Daily View](https://dailyview.org?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

---

### Daily Product Idea

A platform that generates and evaluates software and startup ideas using data from multiple online sources.

Features include:

* AI-generated business ideas
* Trend analysis
* Market attractiveness scoring
* Historical idea archive
* Premium idea claiming

Website: [Daily Product Idea](https://dailyproductidea.com?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

---

### Scarper

A grid-based puzzle game focused on planning, movement and strategy.

Scarper combines simple mechanics with increasingly challenging levels and aims to create satisfying "aha" moments through gameplay.

Website: [Play Scarper](https://www.playscarper.com?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

2. Create a simple prototype
3. Test with real users

## Philosophy

Many successful products start with a hypothesis rather than a certainty.

The goal is not to build the perfect product on day one.

The goal is to learn quickly.

As Reid Hoffman famously observed:

> "If you're not embarrassed by the first version of your product, you've launched too late."
